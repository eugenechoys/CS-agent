# Policy: Channel Selection

```text
Channel decision guidelines for communications planning:

WhatsApp: Best for high-attention moments that should feel direct and personal. Use for important launches, survey invites, or moments that need stronger visibility.

Email: Best for formal launches, summaries, wrap-ups, and messages that should feel official. Use for launch framing, manager reports, and structured updates.

Slack: Works well for workday nudges and lightweight reminders inside the flow of work. Use for midweek check-ins, participation prompts, and low-friction engagement.

Teams: A strong in-work channel for reminders and participation prompts. Use similarly to Slack when the company primarily uses Teams.

Browser Extension: Good for ambient nudges and in-product reminders. Use for contextual prompts, micro-breaks, and non-intrusive wellness cues.

Decision rules:
- High importance + before phase or survey → WhatsApp
- After phase or launches, summaries, reports → Email
- Nudges and reminders → Slack
- Micro or ambient prompts → Browser Extension
- Default fallback → Teams
- Choose fewer, better-timed messages over noisy over-communication.
```
